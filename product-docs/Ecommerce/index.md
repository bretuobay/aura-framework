## 1. Beyond Static Collision Handling: Adaptive Semantic ID Learning for Multimodal Recommendation at Industrial Scale
- **File**: `2604.23522v1.pdf`
- **Authors**: Yongsen Pan, Yuxin Chen, Zheng Hu, Xu Yuan, Daoyuan Wang, Yuting Yin, Songhao Ni, Hongyang Wang, Jun Wang, Fuji Ren, Wenwu Ou
- **Abstract**: Modern recommendation systems operate over massive catalogs of multimodal items whose semantics strongly affect downstream recommendation quality, making scalable item identification a representation problem that must balance compactness for efficient recommendation with semantic fidelity. Semantic IDs (SIDs) offer a practical solution by mapping multimodal item signals into short discrete token sequences, providing a compact yet semantically informed item interface for retrieval, ranking, and emerging generative recommendation. This paper proposes AdaSID, an adaptive semantic ID learning framework that regulates collisions through a two-stage adaptive process: first adaptively relaxing repulsion for each observed overlap based on semantic compatibility, then adaptively allocating regulation pressure according to local collision load and training progress. Extensive offline and online experiments validate AdaSID, showing improvements in Recall and NDCG by around 4.5% on average over strong baselines, while also improving codebook utilization and SID diversity.

## 2. Clarifying or Complicating?: Understanding Older Adults' Engagement with Real-World XAI in E-Commerce
- **File**: `3772318.3791908.pdf`
- **Authors**: Seo Hyeong Kim, Esther Hehsun Kim, Huiyeon Yang, Joonhwan Lee, Hajin Lim
- **Abstract**: E-commerce platforms increasingly deploy explainability features to address concerns about algorithmic opacity. However, most XAI research has focused on younger, tech-savvy users, leaving open questions about how older adults engage with these features in everyday shopping. This paper presents a qualitative study with 20 older adults aged 60+ who regularly use NAVER Shopping, examining their engagement with global (system-level) explanations, local (item-level) explanations, and a user-model dashboard. Findings reveal that explainability does not operate uniformly: many participants did not notice explanation features during routine use or mistook them for advertisements. After guided interaction, global explanations elicited polarized responses, while local explanations helped recalibrate skepticism, and a user-model dashboard exposed tensions between empowerment and surveillance. The authors propose actionable design strategies for building inclusive and adaptive XAI systems for older adults.

## 3. From Navigation to Intention: Reframing the Web Experience through Goal-Driven Interfaces
- **File**: `3774904.3792479.pdf`
- **Authors**: Luca Cordioli, Maristella Matera
- **Abstract**: The dominant paradigm for interacting with the Web has long relied on menu-driven navigation and complex graphical structures that impose a high cognitive load on users. This paper introduces Mirage, a framework that bridges conversational and graphical paradigms by making GUI components interpretable and orchestrable by LLMs, enabling on-the-fly construction of simplified UIs tailored to user requests. Developers declare application functions as reusable components with semantic descriptions and typed schemas; Mirage maps user goals, expressed in natural language, to the most relevant components and renders them as interactive interfaces. Implemented as a Next.js framework and validated in a user study, results show that Mirage significantly reduces task completion time, lowers cognitive workload, and achieves higher usability without compromising accuracy.

## 4. AI-Driven Multi-Agent Shopping System through E-Commerce System
- **File**: `4_AI-Driven+Multi-Agent+Shopping+System+through+E-Commerce+System.pdf`
- **Authors**: Bhuvaneswari Chanamalla, Vemula Nithin Murali, Bandari Suresh, Middela Satya Deepak, Mohammad Zakriya, D. Bhagyaraj Yadav, M. Saravanan
- **Abstract**: This paper proposes a novel AI-driven multi-agent online shopping system that revolutionizes the e-commerce paradigm through autonomous agents capable of negotiation, collaboration, and intelligent decision-making. The system employs specialized agents including Personal Shopping Agents, Price Negotiation Agents, Quality Verification Agents, Logistics Optimization Agents, and Dispute Resolution Agents that work collaboratively to enhance the shopping experience. By integrating machine learning algorithms, natural language processing, federated learning for privacy preservation, and blockchain-based identity verification, the system delivers superior personalization, dynamic pricing, enhanced security, and improved customer satisfaction. Experimental results demonstrate a 67% reduction in purchase decision time, 34% cost savings through automated negotiations, and 89% user satisfaction improvement compared to traditional e-commerce platforms.

## 5. Conversational AI-Powered Fraud Prevention in Augmented Reality E-Commerce: A Natural Language Processing Framework for Real-Time Transaction Security
- **File**: `ConversationalAI-poweredfraudpreventioninaugmentedrealitye-commerce-Anaturallanguageprocessingframeworkforreal-timetransactionsecurity.pdf`
- **Authors**: Oluwadayo Mafolasere Olaniyi, Olubukola Omolara Adebiyi, Cornelia Ifeoma Ejoh, Oluwabukola Oluwaseun Afolabi, Valerie Ojinika Ejiofor
- **Abstract**: This study develops and validates a conversational AI–powered fraud prevention framework for real-time transaction security in augmented reality (AR) e-commerce environments. Adopting an explanatory sequential mixed-methods methodology, the research integrates architectural framework design, quantitative machine learning evaluation, and qualitative trust analysis to address limitations of transaction-only fraud systems in immersive commerce. Publicly available credit card transaction datasets were augmented with synthetically generated AR conversational logs, enabling the modeling of sentiment, behavioral, and temporal context during checkout interactions. A stacking ensemble architecture combining fine-tuned BERT, XGBoost, and Random Forest classifiers was implemented and evaluated under strict real-time constraints.

## 6. Determinants of AI-Integrated E-Commerce Acceptance: The Roles of Personal Innovativeness, Self-Efficacy, Compatibility, and Curiosity
- **File**: `Human Behavior and Emerging Technologies - 2026 - Hien - Determinants of AI‐Integrated E‐Commerce Acceptance  The Roles of.pdf`
- **Authors**: Nguyen Ngoc Hien, Danh Xuan Luu, Tran Nha Ghi, Thuc Ngoc Nguyen
- **Abstract**: Using an integrated model that merges the Technology Acceptance Model with personal innovativeness, compatibility, self-efficacy, and curiosity, this study investigates the primary drivers of users' adoption intentions toward AI-integrated e-commerce platforms in Vietnam. A purposive sample of 401 consumers with AI-integrated shopping experience was surveyed. Structural equation modeling results indicate that personal innovativeness, compatibility, and self-efficacy positively influence both perceived ease of use and perceived usefulness. These perceptions significantly affect users' attitudes and curiosity, which subsequently indirectly determine their intention to utilize AI-integrated platforms. The study provides practical implications for businesses to optimize customer experience, enhance acceptance, and foster AI adoption in Vietnam's e-commerce sector.

## 8. Hybrid GPT and Neural Models for Personalized E-Commerce: A Novel Framework for Adaptive and Transparent Product Recommendations
- **File**: `itegam,+2874+Final+Article-corrigido+ok_pagenumber.pdf`
- **Authors**: Pavan Gunda, Thirupathi Rao Komati
- **Abstract**: This study presents a product recommendation system that integrates OpenAI's GPT model with neural models to transform e-commerce customization. Traditional systems relying on collaborative filtering or content-based models lack precision and adaptability to individual needs. The proposed method overcomes these limitations by integrating hierarchical classification, hybrid similarity scoring, and GPT-based annotation capabilities. The multi-step technique processes textual product descriptions and generated metadata, while embeddings built from neural models provide rich semantic knowledge about relationships between products. A weighted scoring mechanism dynamically adjusts to individual choices, and GPT-inspired factors promote transparency by explaining recommendations in natural language. Experimental results validated the system's ability to offer advanced customization and user satisfaction.

## 9. The Impact of Integrated AI and AR in E-Commerce: The Roles of Personalization, Immersion, and Trust in Influencing Continued Use
- **File**: `jtaer-21-00033.pdf`
- **Authors**: Jingyuan Hu, Eunmi Tatum Lee
- **Abstract**: Digital retail is undergoing a paradigm shift driven by the deep integration of AI and augmented reality (AR). This study develops an integrated model grounded in the stimulus–organism–response (S-O-R) framework and trust transfer theory, examining how personalized recommendation influences users' cognitive state (perceived usefulness) and experiential state (immersion), and how platform trust moderates these effects. Based on survey data from 400 Chinese consumers with AR shopping experience on Taobao, analyzed using structural equation modeling, results indicate that personalized recommendation positively affects both immersion and perceived usefulness; platform trust significantly moderates these effects; and both cognitive and experiential states stimulate positive emotions that enhance continued usage intention. A key finding is a significant positive correlation between perceived usefulness and immersion, revealing the coupling of psychological paths in an integrated AI–AR environment.

## 10. AI Models for Demographic Prediction in E-Commerce: Age and Gender from Initial User Interactions
- **File**: `peerj-cs-3563.pdf`
- **Authors**: Javier De Andrés, Daniel Fernandez-Lanvin, Martin Gonzalez-Rodriguez, Beatriz Pariente-Martinez
- **Abstract**: This research investigates the feasibility of inferring key demographic features—specifically gender and age—from analyzing the performance of fundamental interactive tasks by anonymous users of an e-commerce site. A dataset of interaction patterns from 592 volunteers, encompassing tasks such as Point & Click, Drag & Drop, Text Selection, Text Editing, and Menu Item Selection, was collected and analyzed. Various AI models were trained to identify predictive correlations between task execution times and user demographics. Evaluation using AUC-PR metrics demonstrated the models' efficacy in automatic user profiling, revealing the potential for highly accurate gender determination and age group estimation. These findings highlight the significant potential of AI to create systems for automatically inferring user demographics from initial interactions, enabling dynamic and personalized e-commerce experiences.

## 11. Navigating Trust in Cross-Border E-Commerce: A Systematic Review of Cultural and Consumer Dynamics
- **File**: `s41599-026-06579-4_reference.pdf`
- **Authors**: Yuanyuan Dong
- **Abstract**: Cross-border e-commerce (CBEC) has grown exponentially since 2000, presenting unprecedented opportunities for businesses and consumers. This study systematically reviews the literature, presenting a bibliometric analysis of 773 publications from 2000 to 2024 that indicate critical trends, debates, and knowledge gaps. It shows a shift away from conventional trust mechanisms toward emerging technologies such as blockchain, AI, and real-time data analytics for enhanced transparency and minimized risk. Relational trust-building mechanisms vary significantly across collectivist and individualist cultures. The paper proposes a new framework linking trust-building mechanisms with cultural dimensions and provides practical recommendations for e-commerce platforms to build culturally adaptive consumer trust. Future research should focus on longitudinal studies and neglected cultural aspects.

## 12. AI-Driven Global Commerce: Transforming Digital Banking, Fintech, and E-Commerce
- **File**: `ssrn-6305738.pdf`
- **Authors**: S. Anthony Rahul Golden
- **Abstract**: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is redefining the structure, speed, and scale of global commerce by transforming digital banking systems, fintech innovation, and e-commerce platforms. Through machine learning, predictive analytics, natural language processing, and intelligent automation, AI enhances operational efficiency, financial inclusion, risk management, consumer personalization, and supply chain optimization. This conceptual paper examines AI as a multidimensional technological force reshaping financial intermediation and digital trade, drawing from innovation theory, digital transformation frameworks, and institutional economics. It analyzes structural shifts occurring in global commercial ecosystems, identifies emerging research gaps, governance challenges, and strategic implications for policymakers and business leaders. The paper argues that sustainable AI-driven commerce requires balancing technological innovation with regulatory accountability, ethical standards, and inclusive growth.
